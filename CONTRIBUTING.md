# Editing the TUUCAN website

Welcome! This guide is for **anyone who wants to update the words or pictures on
[tuucan.org](https://tuucan.org)** — no coding or technical experience needed. If you can
fill out a form online, you can edit this website.

Everything you change is saved automatically, goes live within about a minute, and can
always be undone — so don't worry about breaking anything.

---

## What you need (one-time setup)

You need two things before your first edit. You only do this once.

1. **A free GitHub account.** GitHub is the service that safely stores the website and
   keeps a history of every change. Sign up at <https://github.com/signup> if you don't
   already have one. (It's free; you just need an email address.)

2. **To be added as an editor.** Once you have a GitHub account, send your **GitHub
   username** to the site administrator — **Caren Spencer-Smith** — and ask to be added
   as an editor. She'll send you an email invitation from GitHub; click **Accept** in
   that email, and you're set.

> *Note:* the **site administrator** (Caren) handles who can edit the website. This is
> different from the **public contact** for TUUCAN itself, which is Mark Mohundro
> (shown on the site's Get Involved and footer links).

That's it. From now on you can edit anytime.

---

## How to make an edit

1. **Go to the editor:** open **<https://tuucan.org/admin>** in your web browser.

2. **Sign in:** click **“Sign in with GitHub.”** The first time, GitHub will ask you to
   approve — click **Authorize**. (After that it remembers you.)

3. **Open the page content:** click **Site Content**, then **Homepage**. You'll see a
   form with all the editable text laid out in sections.

4. **Make your changes.** Click into any box and type. The sections are:

   | Section | What it controls |
   |---|---|
   | **Site** | The short name, full network name, and main contact email |
   | **Hero (top banner)** | The big headline and the sentence under it at the top of the page |
   | **Shared values** | The list of values (Justice, Equity, …) shown in the strip |
   | **Who We Are** | The heading and the paragraphs in the "Who We Are" section |
   | **Stats** | The three highlighted numbers (e.g. "14 congregations") |
   | **What We Do** | The heading and the cards (Legislative Advocacy, etc.) |
   | **Get Involved** | The call-to-action heading, text, button, and note |
   | **Footer** | The tagline and contact email at the bottom of the page |

5. **Save it:** click **Save** at the top of the editor. This publishes your change.

6. **See it live:** wait about a minute for the site to update, then refresh
   **tuucan.org**. Your edit is now live for everyone.

---

## Adding or changing a photo

Inside an image field, use the **upload button** to pick a picture from your computer.
After you save, the new image appears on the site just like text changes do. Use
reasonably sized photos (a few MB or less) so pages load quickly.

---

## Good things to know

- **You can't permanently break it.** Every save is recorded with your name and the date,
  and any change can be rolled back by an administrator. If something looks off, just fix
  it and save again — or ask for help.
- **Small, frequent edits are great.** You don't have to change everything at once.
- **Multiple people can edit.** Just coordinate so two people aren't editing the exact
  same section at the same moment.
- **Lost or confused?** Contact the site administrator. Nothing you do is final until you
  click Save, and even then it's reversible.
